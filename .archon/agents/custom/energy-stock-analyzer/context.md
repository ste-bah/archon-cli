# Energy Sector Domain Context

## Sector Classification
Energy sector stocks include: Integrated Oil & Gas (XOM, CVX), E&P (EOG, PXD), Oil Services (SLB, HAL),
Midstream/Pipeline (WMB, KMI, ET), Refining (VLO, MPC), Renewables/Clean Energy (FSLR, ENPH), Utilities
with energy exposure, Coal, and Uranium.

## Key Metrics by Sub-Industry
- **Integrated/E&P**: Reserve replacement ratio, finding & development cost/boe, production growth, breakeven $/bbl
- **Midstream**: Distributable cash flow (DCF), coverage ratio, contract duration, take-or-pay %
- **Refining**: Crack spread, utilization rate, Nelson Complexity Index
- **Services**: Rig count correlation, backlog/revenue ratio, day rates

## Valuation Benchmarks (Energy Sector)
- P/E: Sector median ~10-14x (lower than S&P 500 due to cyclicality)
- EV/EBITDA: 4-8x typical for E&P, 8-12x for midstream
- Dividend yield: 2-5% typical; MLPs/royalty trusts may be 6-10%
- FCF yield: >8% considered strong for energy

## Technical Analysis Context
- Energy stocks often move with crude oil (WTI/Brent) -- check correlation
- High beta vs S&P 500 during oil price swings
- Seasonal patterns: driving season demand (Q2-Q3), winter heating (Q4-Q1)
- OPEC announcements can override technical signals

## Regulatory/ESG Considerations
- Methane emission regulations (EPA)
- Carbon tax exposure by geography
- Renewable portfolio standards affecting utilities
- Pipeline permitting challenges (FERC, state-level)
